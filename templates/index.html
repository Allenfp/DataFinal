About this Project

Given how common depression really is, its surprising that there aren’t more ways to indicate its presence in ones life. Some individuals with suicidal tendencies share posts and comments in Social Media and these could be digital clues. We used machine learning to try to indicate it’s presence in texts wherever possible. Our tool aims to assist and identify such tendencies and help prevent potential tragedies.

Team members: Allen, Jenny, Mehreen, and Rommel.

Methodology

We used Natural Language Processing on text scraped from subreddit sr/SuicideWatch and r/CasualConversations. After cleaning it we performed a sentiment analysis on it and took out outliers, and then trained a predictive model to recognize if a text indicates the author's happiness or sadness.

Tools used

Analysis:

PySpark, Praw, NLTK, VADER Sentiment Analysis, Pandas, WTForms

Visualization:

Wordcloud, Bootstrap, HTML, CSS

Limitations

Below are some of the challenges and limitations we came across in our project.

  • Text needs to be of slightly personal nature.
  • Short sentences can yield incorrect predictions.
  • Strong words such as "happy" and "excited" can skew the results.
